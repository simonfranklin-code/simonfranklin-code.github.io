Following Up After Industry Events and Conferences: How it Relates to Digital Marketing.

Attending industry events and conferences is a great way to stay up-to-date with the latest trends and developments in your industry, network with peers, and potentially find new business opportunities. However, attending these events is only half the battle – the real work comes in following up after the event. In this article, we’ll explore the importance of following up after industry events and conferences, and how it relates to digital marketing.

Why Follow Up?

First, let’s discuss why following up is so important. Many attendees make the mistake of thinking that their work is done once the event is over, but in reality, the follow-up process is just as critical. Here are a few reasons why:

  1. Build relationships:
    One of the primary benefits of attending industry events and conferences is the opportunity to meet new people and build relationships with peers and potential partners. Following up after the event is a crucial step in nurturing these relationships and keeping the lines of communication open.
  2. Maximize ROI:
    Attending industry events and conferences can be expensive, so it’s important to get the most out of your investment. Following up with new contacts and leads can help you maximize the return on your investment by turning those connections into tangible business opportunities.
  3. Maintain momentum:
    Attending industry events and conferences can be energizing, but it’s easy to lose momentum once you’re back in the office. Following up with new contacts and leads can help you maintain that energy and momentum, and turn the inspiration from the event into action.

How to Follow Up

Now that we’ve established why following up is important, let’s discuss how to do it effectively. Here are a few tips to keep in mind:

  1. Send a personalized message:
    When following up with new contacts and leads, it’s important to personalize your message as much as possible. Reference specific conversations or topics that you discussed at the event, and show that you’re genuinely interested in continuing the conversation.
  2. Provide value:
    In your follow-up message, try to provide some sort of value to the recipient. This could be a helpful resource or article, an invitation to connect on LinkedIn, or even just a friendly piece of advice related to their industry.
  3. Use a multi-channel approach:
    In addition to email, consider using other channels to follow up with new contacts and leads. This could include social media, phone calls, or even handwritten notes. Using multiple channels can help you stand out from the crowd and increase the chances of getting a response.

How it Relates to Digital Marketing

Finally, let’s discuss how following up after industry events and conferences relates to digital marketing. In today’s digital age, the follow-up process can extend far beyond just sending an email or making a phone call. Here are a few ways that digital marketing can enhance your follow-up efforts:

  1. Social media:
    Following up with new contacts and leads on social media can help you stay top-of-mind and continue the conversation in a more casual setting. Consider connecting with them on LinkedIn or Twitter, and engaging with their posts or sharing relevant content.
  2. Email marketing:
    If you’ve collected email addresses from attendees at an industry event or conference, consider adding them to your email list and sending them targeted follow-up messages. This can help you nurture those leads and turn them into customers over time.
  3. Retargeting:
    If you met someone at an industry event or conference and they visited your website or engaged with your social media profiles, consider retargeting them with display ads or social media ads. This can help you stay on their radar and increase the chances of converting them into a customer.

Conclusion

In conclusion, following up after industry events and conferences is just as important as attending them in the first place. It provides an opportunity to build relationships, maximize your ROI, and maintain momentum. When following up, it's essential to personalize your message, provide value, and use a multi-channel approach. Digital marketing can also enhance your follow-up efforts by leveraging social media, email marketing, and retargeting. By putting these strategies into action, you can turn your industry event and conference attendance into tangible business opportunities and grow your network and influence in your industry.
